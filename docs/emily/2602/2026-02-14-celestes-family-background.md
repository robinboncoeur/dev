## 2. “Her past hitting her in the spine” – what does that *look* like?

We want a moment where:

* Celeste is confronted with a pattern from her past,
* realises she is starting to replicate it with Charli,
* and it hurts enough to change her behaviour.

Here are **three possible shapes** you can blend, tweak, or steal from.

---

### Option A: The father echo

**Core idea:** Celeste realises she’s doing to Charli, emotionally, what her father did to her in absence: making someone’s life revolve around *her* needs.

**Scene-ish sketch:**

* Setting: maybe late-night at Wardrobe or on the marginally seaworthy boat. They’re planning, dreaming, talking about the future.
* Charli blurts something like:

  > “I just want to be what you need, Celeste. Tell me what to be and I’ll… I’ll get there.”
* For a beat, Celeste is thrilled. This is everything she wanted: devotion, trust, a malleable future.
* Then: *spine hit*.
  A memory surfaces. Not a melodramatic flashback, just a line from her father (or about her father):

  * “He always wanted you to be the proof that he wasn’t a failure.”
    or
  * Her mother once saying: “He wanted a daughter who made him look good. He didn’t stick around long enough to meet you.”
* The symmetry knocks her breath sideways:

  * *Oh no. I’m not becoming my father—but I am using someone else’s life to heal my own gap.*

**How Wardrobe Code kicks in:**

* She doesn’t make a grand confession. But next line out of her mouth is *different*:

  * “No, poppet. You don’t exist to be what I need. That’s not what we do here.”
  * “We figure out what makes you feel whole, and I walk alongside. That’s the deal.”
* Later, she might quietly take this to Sarah or Mara:

  * “If I ever start making her small so I can feel big, you tell me. Even if I hate you for it in the moment.”

### Option B: Mirror in someone else’s damage

**Core idea:** Celeste spots a controlling pattern in another relationship—and flinches because it looks like her with Charli.

**Possible carriers:**

**Scene-ish sketch:**

* Celeste is present for this, maybe half-distracted, then suddenly very, very still.
* One line hits her like a physical shove:

**Immediate effect:**

* That night, or later, when she goes to push Charli into some new “experiment” (clothes, pronouns, a social role), she stalls:

  > “I think this would look incredible on you. But… it doesn’t matter what I think if you don’t want it.”
  > “I read you a certain way. I could be wrong. You get to tell me if I am.”

This keeps her decisive, but adds a humility that wasn’t there before.

### Option C: Charli’s trust as indictment
