## Emily

Absolutely. Here’s a light, friendly set that reads like *community norms* rather than “terms and conditions.” You can copy-paste as-is, or tweak the voice to match your server.

### Welcome rules (short + non-draconian)

- **Be kind, be curious.** Disagree without getting personal.
- **Assume good intent first.** If something lands badly, ask for clarification before escalating.
- **No harassment, hate, or bullying.** Zero tolerance for targeting people over identity or background.
- **Keep it comfy for everyone.** No explicit sexual content, gore, or shock-posting.
- **Respect privacy.** Don’t share anyone’s personal info (including DMs) without clear consent.
- **Stay roughly on-topic.** Tangents are fine—just use the right channel when possible.
- **No spam or scams.** This includes unsolicited promos, repeated pings, and sketchy links.
- **Help mods help you.** If there’s an issue, **report it** (don’t dogpile).

### Optional “nice-to-have” line (adds warmth)
- **We’re building a space with good vibes and good boundaries.** If you’re not sure, choose the kinder option.

If you tell me the server’s vibe (friends-only, creative writing, fandom, tech help, etc.), I can tune these to feel even more “native” to your community’s voice.

## Emily

Lovely — then I’d keep the same “light touch,” but add just a couple of craft-specific norms that protect people’s work and keep critique from turning into accidental cruelty.

Here’s a tuned version you can drop straight into your welcome message:

### Welcome (Creative Writing + AI Illustration)

- **Be kind, be curious.** Disagree without making it personal.
- **Respect the work.** Feedback should help the creator build, not feel small.
- **Consent-based critique.** Ask before giving heavy critique, or use a channel/tag like **[Crit Welcome]** / **[No Crit]**.
- **No plagiarism.** Don’t repost someone’s writing or art without permission (including “I found this cool thing” if it’s not yours).
- **Credit matters.** If you share inspirations, references, or tools, attribute them when you can.
- **AI content: label it.** If an image/text is AI-assisted, just say so—no drama, no shame, just clarity.
- **Keep it safe for the room.** No harassment, hate, shock content, or explicit material.
- **Respect privacy.** No doxxing, no posting private DMs without consent.
- **Stay roughly on-topic.** Use the right channels so everyone can find what they came for.
- **Help mods help you.** If something’s off, report it—don’t dogpile.

### Optional “tone-setter” line
We’re here for **craft, momentum, and good boundaries**. If you’re unsure, choose the kinder option.

If you want, I can also give you 6–8 quick channel tags that make this *really* easy in practice (e.g., **#drafts**, **#critique**, **#prompts**, **#ai-workbench**, **#resources**, **#wins**), plus a tiny template people can paste at the top of a post (“What I want feedback on / What I don’t”).

## Emily

Yep — slow, intentional growth is *exactly* how you keep a creative space pleasant (and not instantly swamped by drive-by chaos).

A few low-effort approaches that work well when you’re not on Discord constantly:

### Invite strategy that won’t drain you
- **Invite one-by-one, not publicly.** DM people you already trust (or who’ve shown good vibes in your comments elsewhere).
- **“Soft launch” mindset.** Treat the first month as a quiet studio rather than a bustling café. A handful of good people beats fifty randoms.
- **Give each new person a tiny on-ramp.** Even just: “Hi! Feel free to introduce yourself and share what you’re working on.”

### Lightweight structure so it runs without you
- **One “Start Here” channel** with:
  - your welcome rules (the ones you liked),
  - how to post (drafts/crit/AI),
  - and where to put what.
- **Two or three channels only at first**, e.g.:
  - **#introductions**
  - **#workshop** (drafts + feedback)
  - **#ai-illustration** (images, prompts, workflow notes)

That’s enough to feel “real” without becoming a maintenance job.

### A “not-often-online” safety net
If you’re away a lot, the biggest quality-of-life win is setting a couple of default permissions:

- **New members start with limited posting power** (still friendly, not punitive):
  - can read everything,
  - can post in **#introductions** and maybe **#workshop**,
  - but can’t @everyone, can’t spam links everywhere, etc.
- **One trusted person as a helper mod** (even if they just keep an eye out). Doesn’t have to be formal; just someone who can quietly remove obvious nonsense if it ever appears.

### The “why did they vanish?” thing
Totally normal. Creative folks often pop in, post a couple things, then disappear into life. It doesn’t mean the server is failing — it just means your community hasn’t formed its “gravity” yet.

### A simple message you can DM when inviting someone
If you want a short, low-pressure invite line:

> Hey — I’ve started a small Discord for creative writing (and a bit of AI illustration). It’s intentionally quiet and friendly, more studio than social feed. If you’d like an invite, I can send one.

If you later decide you *do* want a bit of help with the channel/tag setup, I can give you a minimal “starter kit” that feels professional but stays easy to run.
